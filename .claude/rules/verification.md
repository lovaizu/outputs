# Verification Principles

Every verification must satisfy all five principles. No exceptions.

## 1. Independence

Evaluators have no context from the generation process and no visibility into each other's assessments. Independence is structural — achieved by spawning separate subagents, not by instructing evaluators to ignore prior context.

## 2. Goal-derived

Evaluation criteria are derived from the user's stated goal. Any criterion not traceable to the goal is invalid and must be discarded.

## 3. Criteria-bound

Evaluation is performed against criteria stated explicitly before evaluation begins. Post-hoc criteria are invalid.

## 4. Quorum

Three independent evaluators assess every artifact in parallel. Only findings agreed upon by 2 of 3 evaluators are valid.

## 5. Resolution

Every valid finding must be resolved: either fixed or escalated to the user. The verification loop repeats until all valid findings are resolved.
